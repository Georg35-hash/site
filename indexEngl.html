Control Block

Includes an antenna for communication with Earth, a telescope control center, and aiming scopes for targeting objects.

Energy and Control

Solar panels are always directed towards the Sun, while the flap stabilizes the spacecraft's position in space.

Multilayer Sunshield

Five layers of protection shield the telescope from overheating and radiation from the Sun and Earth. It is comparable in size to a tennis court. This feature distinguishes it from other telescopes.

Scientific Instruments Module

A compartment for storing instruments and cameras. Since the James Webb telescope is about 1.6 million kilometers away from Earth, any malfunction would be impossible to repair.

Secondary Mirror

Reflects the signal from the primary mirror to instruments and detectors.

Main Mirror

18 hexagonal segments, each 6.5 meters wide and weighing 40 kg, made of beryllium and coated with 24-carat gold to enhance reflectivity. It captures weak infrared radiation to observe the birth of planets and stars.
